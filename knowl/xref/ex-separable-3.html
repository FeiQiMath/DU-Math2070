Example 1.6.3. Solve the ODE \(y' = xy^3(1 + x^2)^{-1/2}\).

  • Separate the variables:
    \begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dx} = y^3 \cdot \frac{x}{\sqrt{1 + x^2}} \implies \frac{dy}{y^3} = \frac{x}{\sqrt{1 + x^2}}dx \end{equation*}
  • Integrate both sides:
    \begin{equation*} \int \frac{dy}{y^3} = \int \frac{x}{\sqrt{1 + x^2}}dx \implies -\frac{1}{2}y^2 = \sqrt{1 + x^2} + C \end{equation*}
We thus obtain a one-parameter family of implicit solutions
